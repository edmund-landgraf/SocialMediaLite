New users

How to use as a first-time user

SocialMediaLite is like a simplified Facebook profile page — not a full social network feed. This guide assumes someone already hosts the app; you only need a browser.

1. Open the app and sign in

  1. Go to the login page.
  2. Continue with Facebook test — quick demo as @testuser (good for trying the UI on a dev server).
  3. Continue with Facebook — real Facebook sign-in when the host has configured a Meta app.
  4. Other stub test users are listed on the login card for multi-user demos.

After sign-in you land on your profile at /{your-username}.

2. Your profile page

  • Banner — upload a wide image on your own page (when you are the owner).
  • Profile photo — stub “FB frame” in Phase 1; real photos come from Facebook login or uploads elsewhere.
  • Timeline — your posts, newest first; one post can be pinned at the top.
  • Friends list — sidebar links to friends’ pages (no global feed).

3. Create a post

Use the composer on your page (or a friend’s page after you are friends):

  • Text — plain posts; you can style background, text color, and font size.
  • Photo — upload an image (compressed to about 500KB); optional caption.
  • Video link / Web link — paste a URL; the app builds a preview card (title, description, image when available).

4. Friends

  1. Visit someone’s page at /{username}.
  2. Send a friend request; they accept from their page.
  3. Once accepted, you can see their full timeline and post on their page (like a Facebook wall).
  4. Non-friends see a restricted view until the friendship is accepted.

5. Comments and reactions

  • Comment on any post you can see — thread opens under the post.
  • Reaction — pick Like, Love, Care, Agree, Disagree, Celebrate, or Funny from the picker.
  • Disagree can optionally include a short explanation.

6. Friends feed (your page only)

On your own profile, switch to the Friends tab to review posts friends shared to your feed:

  • Unread — new items to read, save, or discard.
  • Read / Saved / Discarded — buckets to organize what you have seen.
  • When you create a post, you can choose to share it to friends’ feeds.

7. Import from Facebook (optional)

On your profile, Import from Facebook copies posts from your Facebook timeline into SocialMediaLite. Login alone does not grant this — the first time you import you may need to Connect Facebook for import (user_posts permission). If you already connected in this browser session, the app reuses your token.

8. Other pages

  • Blog — release-style notes from the project repo.
  • Feedback — suggestions and discussion (pick a display identity).

Quick tips

  • URLs in photo captions become clickable links.
  • Pin only one post on your page at a time.
  • Use Logout from your profile when you are done on a shared computer.
